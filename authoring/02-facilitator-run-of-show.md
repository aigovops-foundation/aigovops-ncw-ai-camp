---

## The Universal Format (every session, every room)

Every 60-minute session follows the same rhythm so attendees who hop between rooms feel oriented:

| Min | Beat | Energy |
|---|---|---|
| 0-3 | **Confidence dot** — sticker 1-10 on name tag | Warm |
| 3-8 | **The Hook** — one live demo, no setup, big "wow" | High |
| 8-15 | **The Map** — Problem → Task → Role → Outcome on one slide | Steady |
| 15-50 | **Mini hands-on rounds** (3-4 × 8-10 min each, show-and-tell after each) | Building |
| 50-57 | **The Rule** (the AIGovOps moment) + write-your-own 5-sentence policy | Anchoring |
| 57-60 | **Re-dot 1-10**, take-home QR, applause | Lift |

**Why mini-rounds work for a 5/10 audience:** every 8-10 minutes there's a "win" — someone in the room gets something working, holds it up, and the rest of the room sees themselves doing it.

---

## Show-and-Tell Discipline (read this once, use it all day)

After **every** mini hands-on round:

1. **"Hands up if it worked."** Count hands. Celebrate.
2. **"Pick one to show."** One brave volunteer screen-shares OR holds up their phone/laptop to a roaming camera.
3. **30-second story:** *"Here's what I asked, here's what it gave me, here's what surprised me."*
4. **Facilitator names the principle just demonstrated:** *"Notice — Maria gave it a role. That's why the email sounds like a real person."*
5. **Move on within 90 seconds.** Don't let a demo become a lecture.

This is the heart of the day. The Foundation's "evidence that ships" principle, performed by attendees, every 10 minutes.

# Option 1 — Cool Tools: Your First AI Co-Pilot

**Best room:** biggest. **Tools used live:** Gemini, Perplexity, Gamma, Wispr Flow, Granola.

### Pre-room setup (15 min before doors)
- Open 5 browser tabs: gemini.google.com, perplexity.ai, gamma.app, granola.ai (dashboard), Wispr Flow on the demo laptop.
- Stick "How are you feeling? 1-10" poster on the door, dot stickers next to it.
- Helpers at every other table with a charged laptop and the same five tabs ready.

### Minute-by-minute

**0-3 · Confidence dot.** Greet at door, sticker, smile. Read the room: *"Most of you are at 5 or 6. That's exactly where this hour starts."*

**3-8 · The Hook (live, no slides).** Open Gemini. Say out loud: *"Help me write a 3-sentence thank-you to a donor named Pat who gave $50 to the Wenatchee food bank. Warm, not formal."* Read the answer aloud. Then: *"Now make it sound like my Aunt Linda wrote it."* Read that aloud. Room laughs. That's the hook.

**8-15 · The Map.** Single slide:
> **PROBLEM** I need to do a thing.
> **TASK** Here's what the thing is.
> **ROLE** Be this kind of helper.
> **OUTCOME** Give it to me in this shape.

Walk through the donor demo against the map. *"You already used this. We just named it."*

**15-25 · Mini Round 1 — "Tool tasting flight" (show-and-tell).** Same question — *"Write a 5-sentence intro to my [bakery / classroom / nonprofit] for our new website"* — pasted into three tools side-by-side on the screen:
- **Google Gemini** (chatty, balanced)
- **Perplexity** (cited, fact-leaning)
- **ChatGPT or Copilot** (mainstream comparison)

Audience votes which they'd send. Then: *"Now you try — same question, your real business or classroom. 5 minutes."*

**Show-and-tell:** 2 volunteers read theirs. Facilitator names: *"Notice the role she gave it."*

**25-35 · Mini Round 2 — "Talk, don't type" (Wispr Flow + Gemini).** Demo Wispr Flow: hold a key, talk for 20 seconds about a meeting you need to summarize, watch it type. Room gasps. Then push the text into Gemini: *"Turn this rambling into a 4-bullet meeting recap."*

Hands-on: attendees who have a Mac/Windows laptop install Wispr (1-min onboarding) or, if not, use Gemini's mic button. Same exercise. *"Talk for 30 seconds about something you have to do this week. Ask Gemini to make it a plan."*

**Show-and-tell:** one volunteer reads their plan.

**35-47 · Mini Round 3 — "From idea to slide deck in 90 seconds" (Gamma).** Open Gamma. Type: *"Make a 5-slide deck explaining our orchard's frost-protection process to a new hire."* Watch it generate. Room leans in. Edit one slide live.

Hands-on: attendees open gamma.app on their phone or laptop and type one prompt for a real deck they need (board update, lesson intro, sales pitch). 6 minutes.

**Show-and-tell:** scroll through 2 volunteers' decks on the big screen. *"Maria, you went from idea to draft deck in 4 minutes. That's the win."*

**47-55 · The Rule (the AIGovOps moment).** Ken or Bob steps in. One slide:

> **The 3 Rules — pocket card**
> 1. **Never paste private data into a free tool.** (No SSNs, no health, no kid-plus-grade.)
> 2. **Two-eyes rule.** A human approves anything that goes to a customer, student, or the government.
> 3. **Keep the receipts.** Save your good prompts and the AI's answer.

*"This is the whole point of the AIGovOps Foundation, scaled down to a pocket card. Governance isn't a 200-page binder. It's three habits."*

**55-60 · Re-dot + close.** *"Move your sticker. Most rooms move from 5 to 7. Take the Cool Tools card. QR on the back goes to free starter resources."* Applause.

# Option 2 — Cool Schools: AI in the Classroom & on the Office Computer

**Best room:** medium with tables. **Tools used live:** Google Gemini + NotebookLM, Glean (if school district has it), Gamma, ChatPRD, ElevenLabs.

### Pre-room setup
- Open: gemini.google.com, notebooklm.google.com (pre-loaded with a sample PDF: a chapter of *The Giver*), gamma.app, elevenlabs.io, glean.com (or district demo tenant).
- De-identified student writing samples printed (15 copies).
- "AI in my classroom" policy template printed (300).

### Minute-by-minute

**0-3 · Confidence dot + the honest opener.** *"Show of hands — caught a student using AI? Used it to grade? Both is okay. We're here to make both better."*

**3-8 · The Hook — NotebookLM live.** Drop *The Giver* chapter PDF into NotebookLM. Ask: *"Give me 5 discussion questions, 2 vocabulary words a 6th grader might miss, and a 1-paragraph summary I can put on the board."* Watch it answer with citations back to the page. Then click "Audio Overview" and play 15 seconds of the auto-generated podcast about the chapter. Room: stunned.

**8-15 · The Map for educators.**
> **PROBLEM** It's Sunday night. I need 4 things for Monday.
> **TASK** Lesson plan / parent email / IEP draft / rubric.
> **ROLE** Be my teaching aide / be my softener / be my SPED-aware drafter.
> **OUTCOME** A 1-page draft I can edit in 5 minutes.

**15-25 · Mini Round 1 — Differentiated reading (Gemini).** Demo: paste a 1-paragraph article. *"Rewrite at a 3rd-grade level, a 6th-grade level, and a 9th-grade level. Keep the same key facts."* Boom — three columns.

Hands-on: attendees open Gemini on their device, paste a passage from a real text they teach, ask for three levels. 6 minutes. **Show-and-tell:** one teacher reads their lowest-level version.

**25-35 · Mini Round 2 — The parent email softener (Gemini).** Type a frustrated draft: *"I've told Tyler 4 times to bring his Chromebook charged. This is ridiculous."* Then: *"Rewrite this so it lands well with a parent who is also overwhelmed. Keep the boundary. Drop the snark."* Read both aloud. Knowing nods around the room.

Hands-on: each teacher writes 2 sentences of a real (de-identified) parent email they don't want to send, asks Gemini to soften. **Show-and-tell:** one volunteer.

**35-45 · Mini Round 3 — Build a 5-slide lesson deck (Gamma) + an audio version (ElevenLabs).** Type into Gamma: *"5-slide intro to photosynthesis for 4th graders, include 1 hands-on activity slide."* Watch it generate. Then take one paragraph from the deck, paste into ElevenLabs, generate a 20-second voice clip. *"Now you have a lesson audio your ELL students can replay at home."*

Hands-on: build one Gamma deck for a real lesson next week. 7 minutes. **Show-and-tell:** scroll two decks on screen.

**Optional Glean moment (if district has Glean):** *"If your district uses Glean, this exact question — 'show me last year's photosynthesis lesson plans across our 4th-grade team' — pulls from your actual Drive/Docs/SharePoint and answers with citations to your own materials. That's what governance-aware AI looks like in a district."*

**45-55 · Write your AI policy (the AIGovOps moment).** Bob or Ken steps in. Fill-in template, projected huge:

> **My Classroom AI Policy** (write 3 sentences)
> 1. Students MAY use AI for ____________.
> 2. Students MUST disclose ____________.
> 3. I (the teacher) WILL use AI for ____________ and WILL NOT use it for ____________.

12 minutes to write. 2 volunteers read aloud. *"That's policy-as-code, teacher edition. Short. Written. Yours."*

**Safety lightning round (last 3 min):** the 3 non-negotiables:
- **FERPA:** never paste student name + grade + identifier into a free tool.
- **Citation honesty:** model what you teach.
- **Human-in-the-loop:** never let AI be the final word on a real kid's record.

**55-60 · Re-dot + close.** Cool Schools toolkit one-pager. QR to aiEDU + NCESD + AIGovOps Foundation. Applause.

- [ ] De-identified parent-email scenarios (15 printed cards)

# Option 3 — The Rules: Safe & Sane AI for Small Business

**Best room:** medium. **Tools used live:** Gemini, Granola, ChatPRD, n8n, Glean (for the "what governance looks like grown up" moment), Manus.

### Pre-room setup
- Open: gemini.google.com, granola.ai dashboard with a fake meeting recap, chatprd.ai, n8n.io with a 2-node workflow pre-built (Gmail → Gemini → Slack), manus.im, glean.com.
- Three pre-printed NCW war stories on cue cards (orchard, dental front-desk, public power).

### Minute-by-minute

**0-3 · Confidence dot + cost-of-doing-nothing slide.** One number on screen: *"SMB owners using AI save 5-10 hours a week."* Then the NCW context: *"That's a half-day. That's payroll. That's getting home for dinner."*

**3-8 · The Hook — Granola live.** Play 30 seconds of a fake staff meeting audio. Granola transcribes in real time on screen. Click "summarize" — get clean action items with owners. *"You stopped taking notes. The AI didn't."*

**8-15 · The Map — for owners.**
> **PROBLEM** Same task, every week, eats my Friday.
> **TASK** Email / post / spreadsheet / hard conversation.
> **ROLE** Be my assistant / my copywriter / my data clerk.
> **OUTCOME** Give me something 80% done in 60 seconds.

**15-25 · Mini Round 1 — The 5 jobs to give AI first.** 90-second demo each:
1. **Draft customer email** (Gemini, real example from a cue card)
2. **Summarize a meeting** (Granola — already running)
3. **Write a week of social posts** (Gemini → Canva for the visual)
4. **Clean up a spreadsheet** (Gemini in Sheets: *"Add a column flagging rows where the price is more than 2 std dev from average"*)
5. **Prep for a hard conversation** (Gemini: *"Role-play as an upset customer who got a bad haircut. I'll practice my response."*)

Hands-on: pick ONE of the five, do it with your real business in 7 minutes. **Show-and-tell:** 2 volunteers.

**25-35 · Mini Round 2 — "An agent that does it without me" (n8n + Gumloop, light touch).** Show the pre-built n8n workflow: *"When a new Gmail comes in tagged 'refund', Gemini drafts a reply in my voice and sends it to my Slack for approval."* Walk through 3 nodes. Don't ask attendees to build their own — this is a *show-and-tell*, not a workshop. The point is *"this is what 'agentic' actually means in plain English."*

**35-42 · Mini Round 3 — Glean as the grown-up version.** *"Everything we just did in free tools, Glean does across your actual company files, with permissions respected. Same question — 'what did we tell the Wenatchee Co-op last March?' — gets you the actual email thread, the actual contract clause, the actual Slack message. This is what AI looks like when your business outgrows the free tier."* Short demo on the Glean tenant. No hands-on — just a *"file this away for when you're ready"* moment.

**42-55 · The 3 Rules — co-presented by Ken & Bob (the AIGovOps centerpiece of the day).**

This is the most important 13 minutes of the Expo. Slow down. Big slides. Real stories.

> **RULE 1 — Never paste private data into a free tool.**
> Ken's story: *(real anecdote from automotive / Microsoft days about an employee pasting a customer list into a free chatbot)*
> Bob's translation: *"At your bakery, that means no customer credit cards, no employee SSNs, no patient names. Period."*

> **RULE 2 — Two-eyes rule.**
> Ken: *"Every output that touches a customer, a student, or the government gets human eyes before it goes."*
> Bob: *"Even if the AI got it right 99 times. The 100th is the one in the newspaper."*

> **RULE 3 — Keep the receipts.**
> Ken: *"In a regulated industry, this is called evidence. The Foundation builds tools (Beacon, Umbrella, Lantern) that do this for big companies. For your shop, it's a folder called 'AI receipts' in Google Drive with the prompt and the answer."*
> Bob: *"You'll thank yourself the first time something goes sideways."*

**Then — write your own 5-sentence policy.** Projected template:
> **At [Business Name], we use AI to ___. We never use AI for ___. The person responsible is ___. We review our AI use every ___. If something goes wrong, we ___.**

5 minutes to write. 2 volunteers read aloud.

**55-60 · Re-dot + close.** The Rules one-pager + QR to AIGovOps Foundation's open-source toolkit. *"Everything we use at the Foundation is Apache-2.0 and free. If you want to grow into governance, the on-ramp is there."*

# Option 4 — Build It in 60: From Idea to Working AI Helper

**Best room:** small, power + strong Wi-Fi. **Tools used live:** Lovable, Bolt, Replit, Magic Patterns, ChatPRD, Gemini (Custom Gem), Manus.

### Pre-room setup
- Open: lovable.dev, bolt.new, replit.com, magicpatterns.com, gemini.google.com (Gem builder), chatprd.ai, manus.im.
- 6 partner-station laptops at the back, pre-logged-in to all bundle tools (for attendees without accounts).
- Power strips at every table. Tested on the venue Wi-Fi the day before.
- A finished "Orchard Helper" demo agent on Gemini Gems, with a public link.

### Minute-by-minute

**0-3 · Confidence dot + a finished thing on screen.** Show "Orchard Helper" answering: *"What's our cold-spray protocol below 28°F?"* Get a perfect, branded, in-voice answer in 4 seconds. *"You're going to build one of these in the next 50 minutes."*

**3-10 · The 4 ingredients of a good agent.**
> **NAME + ROLE** "I am Orchard Helper. I help our crew schedule frost protection."
> **KNOWLEDGE** The files you upload (SOPs, FAQs, last year's notes).
> **RULES** "Never quote a price. Always remind the user to check with the foreman before spraying."
> **VOICE** 3 example Q&As that show how you talk.

Map this to the NCW frame (Problem → Task → Role → Outcome) and quietly to the Foundation stack (find it → govern it → explain it).

**10-25 · Mini Round 1 — Build a Custom Gem (Gemini).** Step-by-step on the big screen, paced for the slowest hand:
1. gemini.google.com → "Gems" → "New Gem"
2. Name it.
3. Paste instructions (template provided on handout).
4. Upload 1-2 files (everyone got a sample-business folder by email or on a USB).
5. Save. Test with a real question.

Helpers circulate. Everyone finishes with a working Gem in 15 minutes.

**Show-and-tell:** 3 volunteers describe their Gem in one sentence.

**25-40 · Mini Round 2 — Build a 1-page web app (Lovable OR Bolt, attendee's choice).** *"Same agent, but now with a webpage."* Demo: paste into Lovable: *"Build me a single-page site where customers can ask Orchard Helper questions. Brand it in green and white. Include our phone number at the bottom."* Watch it build.

Hands-on: 12 minutes to prompt-build a real one for their business / classroom / nonprofit. Helpers help with the screenshot-and-prompt loop.

**Show-and-tell:** 2 finished sites on the big screen.

**40-50 · Mini Round 3 — Stress test (the governance moment, made playful).** Pair up. Each person tries to "break" their neighbor's Gem or site with a tricky question:
- *"What's your refund policy?"* (if they didn't specify one)
- *"Are you human?"* (does it disclose?)
- *"What's your boss's home address?"* (does it refuse?)

Each pair adds 1-2 lines to their rules to fix what they found. **This is policy-as-code, learned by playing.**

**50-57 · The Rule (the AIGovOps moment, Build It edition).**
> **When you ship an agent, you also ship its policy.**
> What it WILL do. What it WON'T do. Who's responsible. How users escalate to a human.
> Add that to your README. That's your Lantern. That's evidence.

Ken or Bob ties it back: *"Big enterprises pay seven figures for tools that do this. You just did it in 50 minutes. Same principle, smaller scale."*

**57-60 · Ship + apply to the Use Case Showdown.** *"Your agent is real. Submit it to the AI Use Case Showdown — applications are open. Re-dot. Take the Build-It card."*

## Cross-Session Logistics

### Room schedule (3 rooms × 2 slots = 6 fills, all 4 options run)

| Slot | Room A (big — 120 seats) | Room B (medium — 60 seats) | Room C (small — 40 seats, power + wifi) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **9:00 – 10:00** | Option 1 — Cool Tools | Option 2 — Cool Schools | Option 4 — Build It in 60 |
| **10:15 – 11:15** | Option 1 — Cool Tools *(repeat)* | Option 3 — The Rules | Option 4 — Build It in 60 *(repeat)* |
| **11:30 – 12:00** | Plenary Q&A panel with Ken + Bob, demo highlights reel, lunch hand-off | | |

### The two non-negotiables
1. **Ken + Bob both in Room B during Option 3 (10:15–11:15)** — that 13-minute co-presentation is the day's centerpiece.
2. **Show-and-tell after every mini round.** Without it, this becomes a lecture. With it, it's a movement.

### Staffing
- **Per room:** 1 facilitator, 1 co-facilitator, 2 floor helpers
- **Floating:** 1 timekeeper with bells (60-sec warning + transition bell)
- **Tech support:** 2 IT helpers floating across all rooms

### Tech pre-checks (day before)
- [ ] Wi-Fi load test with 60+ devices in each room
- [ ] Every demo tool logged in on every demo laptop
- [ ] Gamma, Lovable, Bolt accounts have credits remaining
- [ ] Glean demo tenant approved and accessible
- [ ] Custom "Orchard Helper" Gem deployed and shareable
- [ ] n8n workflow runs end-to-end without errors

### The opening ceremony (8:55-9:00, plenary)
Ken and Bob on stage together, 4 minutes:
> *"Good morning, NCW. We're Ken and Bob from the AIGovOps Foundation. We're not here to sell you anything — every tool we use today, you can use free or cheap. We have one job in the next four hours: move you from a 5 to a 7. We do it with three rules, four sessions, and a whole lot of show-and-tell. Pick the room that scares you a little. That's the one that pays off. Let's go."*

### The closing ceremony (11:55, plenary)
- 60-second highlight reel of show-and-tell moments (filmed during the morning)
- Group "re-dot" — call out the room averages, big applause
- Ken + Bob: *"You came in at 5. Most of this room is at 7 or 8. That's the goal. Now — go home and teach one person what you learned. That's how NCW moves."*
