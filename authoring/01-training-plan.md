# Cool Tools, Cool Schools & The Rules
## A Practical AI Training Plan for NCW Tech Alliance
*Designed for the AI Expo 2026 — Wenatchee Convention Center · Up to 300 attendees · 3 breakout rooms · 60-minute sessions*

**Co-presented by:** Ken Johnston & Bob Rapp — AiGovOps Foundation
**Audience:** Educators (K-12, higher-ed, librarians), small-and-medium business owners, nonprofit leaders, and community members across North Central Washington

---

## The North Star

Move every attendee from a self-rated **5/10** ("AI-curious, a little nervous, not sure where to start") to a **7-8/10** ("I have a tool, a workflow, and a guardrail I can use Monday morning") in 60 minutes.

We do that by speaking NCW's existing language — **Problem → Task → Role → Outcome** — and layering in three principles from the AiGovOps Foundation that keep the work safe and trustworthy:

| Foundation idea | What it sounds like for this room |
|---|---|
| Governance runs like engineering | "Have a checklist, not a vibe." |
| Evidence that ships | "Save the prompt, save the answer, save the source." |
| Policy as code | "Write your AI rules down — even one paragraph counts." |

Tone for all four sessions: **practical, fun, engaging, positive.** Live demos beat slides. Real NCW examples (orchards, school districts, clinics, nonprofits, public power) beat generic ones. Everyone leaves with a one-page handout and at least one finished artifact on their own device.

---

## The Four 60-Minute Breakout Options

Pick any three to run in parallel rooms (Sessions 1, 2, 3 → three rooms × two timeslots = six fills, all four can run if you go 2 hours). Each is self-contained and tuned to a different attendee mindset.

### Option 1 — "Cool Tools": Your First AI Co-Pilot
**Best room:** the biggest room. **Best for:** the curious 5/10 majority — first-time users, retirees, civic volunteers, sole proprietors.

### Option 2 — "Cool Schools": AI in the Classroom & on the Office Computer
**Best room:** medium room with tables. **Best for:** K-12 teachers, principals, librarians, district staff, CWU/WVC educators.

### Option 3 — "The Rules": Safe & Sane AI for Small Business
**Best room:** medium room. **Best for:** SMB owners, bookkeepers, nonprofit EDs, HR-of-one, healthcare/insurance/public-power staff who need to *use* AI without breaching privacy or compliance.

### Option 4 — "Build It in 60": From Idea to Working AI Helper
**Best room:** smaller room with strong Wi-Fi and power. **Best for:** the 6-7/10 crowd ready to ship something — entrepreneurs, dev-curious educators, Use Case Showdown hopefuls.

## Option 1 — Cool Tools: Your First AI Co-Pilot

> *"By 10 a.m. you will have used three AI tools, saved 20 minutes of work, and will know which one to open tomorrow morning."*

**Outcome (the 7/10):** Each attendee leaves with one real task done by AI (a drafted email, a summarized PDF, a brainstormed list) saved to their device.

### Run-of-show (60 min)

| Time | Block | What happens |
|---|---|---|
| 0:00 – 0:05 | **Confidence check-in** | Everyone holds up fingers 1-10. Facilitator names the room average. "We're moving you up two." |
| 0:05 – 0:15 | **The 4-part recipe** | Teach the NCW frame: **Problem → Task → Role → Outcome.** Live-demo one prompt with each part missing, then complete. Audience votes which version is better. |
| 0:15 – 0:30 | **Tool tasting flight** | Three 5-minute demos on the screen: ChatGPT (free), Microsoft Copilot (free with a work/school account), Perplexity (free). Same question — "Help me write a thank-you note to a donor who gave $50 to our food bank" — three answers compared. |
| 0:30 – 0:50 | **Your turn (hands on)** | Three challenges, pick one: (a) summarize a 10-page PDF you brought, (b) draft a Facebook post for your business, (c) brainstorm 10 lesson hooks for a topic you teach. Facilitator + two helpers circulate. |
| 0:50 – 0:57 | **Share-outs** | 3 volunteers read what AI gave them. Room applauds. |
| 0:57 – 1:00 | **The one rule** | "Never paste in anything you wouldn't email to a stranger." Take-home card: *Cool Tools Starter Pack* (one-pager with links + the recipe). Re-rate 1-10. |

**Materials needed:** projector, one demo laptop, Wi-Fi, printed one-pagers (300), name tags marked with current 1-10 score (sticker dots).

**Why it lands:** zero jargon, every minute they're either watching magic or making it. The Foundation's "evidence" principle shows up gently: *"save your good prompts in a Word doc — that's your AI cookbook."*

## Option 2 — Cool Schools: AI in the Classroom & on the Office Computer

> *"You'll leave with three lesson assets, one parent-communication template, and a classroom AI policy you actually believe in."*

**Outcome (the 7/10):** Each educator leaves with a working AI workflow for one of: lesson planning, IEP/504 drafting support, parent communications, or assessment rubrics — *plus* a one-paragraph classroom AI use policy in their own voice.

### Run-of-show (60 min)

| Time | Block | What happens |
|---|---|---|
| 0:00 – 0:05 | **The honest opener** | "Show of hands — how many of you have caught a student using AI? How many of you have *used* AI to grade? Both is okay." Normalizes the room. |
| 0:05 – 0:20 | **Four educator wins** | Live demos: (1) differentiated reading passage at 3 levels, (2) IEP goal draft from notes, (3) parent email softened from a frustrated draft, (4) rubric generated from a learning target. Each demo ≤ 3 min. |
| 0:20 – 0:40 | **Build-your-own (pick one)** | Educators open ChatGPT or Copilot on their device and replicate one of the four with their own subject/student/situation. Two CWU-style "lab assistants" roam. |
| 0:40 – 0:52 | **Write the rule** | Guided 12-minute exercise: each teacher writes a 3-sentence "AI in my classroom" policy using a fill-in template (*Students may use AI for ___. Students must disclose ___. I will use AI for ___.*). Two volunteers read theirs aloud. This is the "policy-as-code" idea translated for teachers — your policy is short, written, and you can run it. |
| 0:52 – 0:58 | **Safety & sources** | The 3 non-negotiables: FERPA (never paste student names + grades + identifiers into a free public tool), citation honesty, and human-in-the-loop on anything that touches a real kid's record. |
| 0:58 – 1:00 | **Take-home & re-rate** | *Cool Schools Toolkit* one-pager: 10 prompts, 3 free tools, 1 policy template, links to aiEDU and NCESD resources. |

**Materials needed:** projector, sample (de-identified) student writing for demos, printed policy template (300), small prize for first volunteer to share their policy.

**Why it lands:** every educator gets something *finished* — a draft lesson, a draft policy. The Foundation principle that shows up: **"evidence that ships."** Their lesson plan IS the evidence.

## Option 3 — The Rules: Safe & Sane AI for Small Business

> *"Use AI confidently in your business this month — without leaking customer data, hallucinating numbers, or getting your accountant nervous."*

**Outcome (the 7/10):** Each owner/operator leaves with (a) a list of 3 tasks AI should do for them next week, (b) a one-page "AI use policy" for their team, and (c) clarity on what data *never* goes into a chatbot.

### Run-of-show (60 min)

| Time | Block | What happens |
|---|---|---|
| 0:00 – 0:05 | **The cost of doing nothing** | One slide: SMB owners using AI save an average of 5-10 hours a week. Show one local example (orchard scheduling, real-estate listing, dental front-desk). |
| 0:05 – 0:18 | **The 5 jobs to give AI first** | Demo each in 90 seconds: (1) draft customer emails, (2) summarize meetings, (3) write social posts, (4) clean up spreadsheets, (5) prep for a hard conversation (firing, vendor dispute, refund). |
| 0:18 – 0:35 | **Workshop: my next 3 jobs** | Each attendee fills a worksheet: *Three repetitive tasks in my business. Which AI tool. What could go wrong.* Pair-share with the person next to them. |
| 0:35 – 0:48 | **The 3 Rules (the AiGovOps moment)** | Co-present with Ken or Bob. Three rules, big and friendly on screen: **(1) Never paste customer PII, payment info, or health data into a free tool.** **(2) Two-eyes rule — humans approve anything that goes to a customer or the government.** **(3) Keep the receipts — save your prompts and outputs so you can show your work.** Each rule explained with a 30-second NCW-flavored war story. |
| 0:48 – 0:57 | **Write your one-page AI policy** | Fill-in template: *"At [Business], we use AI to ___. We never use AI for ___. The person responsible is ___. We review our AI use every ___."* Five sentences. Done. |
| 0:57 – 1:00 | **Take-home & re-rate** | *The Rules* one-pager: the 3 rules, the policy template, a short checklist of approved free + paid tools, and a QR to the AiGovOps Foundation's free open-source resources. |

**Materials needed:** projector, printed worksheets (300), printed policy template, fine-tip pens.

**Why it lands:** business owners are scared of two things — being left behind, and being on the news for the wrong reason. This session solves both in one hour. It's the most direct expression of the Foundation's work: **governance that runs like engineering, scaled down to a five-sentence policy.**

## Option 4 — Build It in 60: From Idea to Working AI Helper

> *"In one hour we'll build a custom AI assistant for your business, classroom, or nonprofit — no code, free tools, yours forever."*

**Outcome (the 7/10):** Each attendee leaves with a working, named **Custom GPT or Copilot Agent** they can use and share — pre-loaded with their context, voice, and rules.

### Run-of-show (60 min)

| Time | Block | What happens |
|---|---|---|
| 0:00 – 0:05 | **Show, don't tell** | Demo a finished agent: "Meet 'Orchard Helper' — knows our SKUs, our tone, our pricing rules." Type one question, get one perfect answer. Room leans in. |
| 0:05 – 0:15 | **The 4 ingredients of a good agent** | **Name + role**, **knowledge** (the files you upload), **rules** (do's and don'ts), **voice** (3 example answers). This maps 1:1 to NCW's *Problem → Task → Role → Outcome* and to the Foundation's **Beacon → Umbrella → Lantern** structure (find it, govern it, explain it) — but we don't say those names, we just use the shape. |
| 0:15 – 0:50 | **Build sprint (35 min, hands on)** | Each attendee opens ChatGPT (Custom GPT builder, free with a paid account, partner-station available for those without) **or** Copilot Studio **or** Claude Projects. Step-by-step screencast on the big screen, paced for the slowest hand. Helpers circulate. Everyone builds: name, instructions, 1-2 uploaded files, 3 example Q&A. |
| 0:50 – 0:55 | **Stress test** | Pair up. Each person tries to "break" their neighbor's agent with a tricky question. Discuss what to add to the rules section. (This is governance, made playful.) |
| 0:55 – 1:00 | **Ship it** | Save link, screenshot, re-rate. Optional: submit your agent to the AI Use Case Showdown. |

**Materials needed:** projector, strong Wi-Fi, 4-6 partner laptops with pre-logged-in accounts for attendees without paid ChatGPT, printed 4-ingredient template (300), power strips at every table.

**Why it lands:** this is the dopamine room. People who come in at 5/10 leave at 8/10 because they're holding a *thing they built*. The Foundation lens is invisible but everywhere — they wrote rules, they tested guardrails, they kept evidence (the chat history).

## How to Schedule Three Breakout Rooms

| Slot | Room A (big) | Room B (medium) | Room C (small, power+wifi) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **9:00 – 10:00** | Option 1 — Cool Tools | Option 2 — Cool Schools | Option 4 — Build It in 60 |
| **10:15 – 11:15** | Option 1 — Cool Tools *(repeat)* | Option 3 — The Rules | Option 4 — Build It in 60 *(repeat)* |
| **11:30 – 12:00** | Plenary regroup, Q&A panel with Ken & Bob, lunch hand-off | | |

This pattern lets all four options run, repeats the two highest-demand ones (Cool Tools, Build It in 60), and keeps every educator and SMB attendee with a path that fits them.

## What Every Attendee Walks Away With

1. **A finished artifact** (a drafted email, a lesson, a policy, or a custom GPT).
2. **A one-page handout** specific to their session.
3. **A 5-sentence AI use policy** in their own words (Options 2, 3, 4).
4. **The 3 Rules card**, pocket-sized, same in every room:
   - Never paste private data into a free tool.
   - Two-eyes rule on anything that goes to a customer, student, or government.
   - Keep the receipts.
5. **A QR code** to a free resource bundle: AiGovOps Foundation open-source toolkit, aiEDU classroom materials, NCW Tech Alliance follow-on workshops, and an "I-want-to-keep-learning" signup.

## Why This Is on Brand for Both Hosts

**For NCW Tech Alliance:** Every session uses their **Problem → Task → Role → Outcome** language, mirrors their existing AI 101/201 ladder, and slots cleanly into the AI Expo morning workshop schedule. It feeds their Use Case Showdown, their AI-Skill-A-Thon pipeline, and their AI in Action follow-up series.

**For the AiGovOps Foundation:** Every session quietly teaches the Foundation's three core ideas — *governance like engineering, evidence that ships, policy as code* — at the scale of one teacher, one orchard, one nonprofit. It's the **mission of the Foundation as stated in the charter: empower disadvantaged, rural, and small communities through improved AI access and impact** — turned into a Tuesday morning in Wenatchee.
