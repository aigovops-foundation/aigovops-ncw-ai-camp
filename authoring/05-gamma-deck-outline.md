# SLIDE 19 — RULE 1: Never paste private data into a free tool.
**No** Social Security numbers.
**No** student names + grades + identifiers.
**No** patient information.
**No** customer credit cards.
**No** contracts marked confidential.
If you wouldn't email it to a stranger, don't paste it into ChatGPT.

---

# SLIDE 20 — RULE 2: The two-eyes rule.
Every output that goes to a **customer**, a **student**, a **regulator**, or **the press** → a human reads it first.
Even when the AI is right 99 times out of 100.
The 100th is the one that costs you.

---

# SLIDE 21 — RULE 3: Keep the receipts.
Make a folder in Drive called **"AI receipts."**
For anything important: paste the prompt + paste the answer.
You'll thank yourself the first time something goes sideways.
*This is what the Foundation calls "evidence that ships" — at your scale.*

# SLIDE 44 — Two ideas that change how we use AI for hard questions
**DEEP THINK** — instead of an instant answer, the AI spends 30 sec to 3 min reasoning, exploring multiple paths, checking its work. Gemini 3, GPT-5 o-series, Claude extended thinking.
**MODEL COUNCIL** — three frontier AIs answer the same question in parallel, a fourth synthesizes. Available in Perplexity Model Council + open-source AI Council Framework.
*The questions that matter most are exactly the ones you should never trust one AI to answer alone.*

2. **First-pass theme:** pick **"Oasis"** or **"Daktilo."** Both have warm, friendly type and good contrast.

5. **Brand the title and closing slide** with NCW Tech Alliance logo + AiGovOps Foundation logo.
