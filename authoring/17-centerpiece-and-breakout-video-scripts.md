## How to Use This Document

Each of the five videos below contains:
- **EN Narration** — English voice-over, word count, estimated run time
- **ES Narración** — Mexican Spanish adaptation (cultural, not literal), same pacing
- **Voice & Avatar** — ElevenLabs voice, Synthesia avatar, stability/similarity settings
- **Storyboard** — Shot-by-shot breakdown with timecodes, visuals, on-screen text, narration cues, Gamma prompts
- **CTA / Ending** — Exact screen text and URL

**Total estimated finished video: ~10.5 minutes across all five productions.**

---

## Production-Wide Notes

**Visual palette (all videos):** Warm amber, sage green, deep river blue — the natural palette of NCW. Orchard rows, rivers, small classrooms, real faces. Never corporate-stock-photo humans. Never cold-tech-blue UI chrome.

**Music architecture:**
- *Centerpiece*: Solo piano → cello → full orchestral arc. Artlist "hopeful orchestral / cinematic / uplifting."
- *Breakouts*: Upbeat but grounded — acoustic guitar + light percussion. Artlist "acoustic indie / community / warm." Each breakout uses the same underlying track for event coherence.

**Language note:** The Spanish versions are adapted into the Mexican Spanish spoken by working families in the Wenatchee Valley, Cashmere, Omak, and Brewster. "Usted" in formal pledge contexts; "tú" for student/peer moments. "Guardar el rastro" = "keep the receipts." Agricultural and orchard references used where natural. Jake's name is kept as-is (real name in real US schools); María is the same character.

# VIDEO 2 — COOL TOOLS BREAKOUT INTRO
## "Your First AI Co-Pilot" · 90 seconds · Tool-tasting flight hook

*Audience: the 5/10 confidence majority — curious, slightly nervous, "I've heard of ChatGPT but I've never really used it." Room A — 120 seats.*

---

## A. EN Narration

**Target:** ~190 words · ~90 seconds at warm-conversational pace

---

> **[VOICE — upbeat, friendly, like a good coworker showing you something cool]**
> You've heard of AI. Maybe you've poked at ChatGPT once or twice.
> Maybe you figured it wasn't really for you.
>
> Wrong room for that thought.
> Right room for what's next.

> **[VOICE — brisk, energetic]**
> In the next hour, you're going to taste-test five AI tools
> the way you'd taste-test five hot sauces at a market booth —

> **[VOICE — demo energy]**
> You're going to watch a single sentence
> turn into a thank-you email your donor actually wants to read.

> and watch AI hand you back a plan.
> You're going to go from a blank slide to a five-slide deck
> in four minutes — on your phone if that's all you've got.

> **[VOICE — warm, the invite]**
> You came in at a five.
> You're leaving at a seven.
> That's the whole deal.

> **[VOICE — the close — punchy]**
> Room A. Starts right now.
> Bring whatever you've got — phone, laptop, tablet.
> Your first AI co-pilot is waiting.
