# HANDOUT A — The NCW AI Partnership Pledge (v1.0)

## FRONT

### 🤝 The NCW AI Partnership Pledge
**Signed by the citizens, educators, business owners, and community leaders of North Central Washington · AI Expo 2026 · August 11**

We believe AI can be the most powerful partner for learning, work, and community impact this region has ever had — **if we do it right.** This is what we commit to.

**1. AI is a partner, not a replacement.**
We never let AI be the final word on a student's learning, a worker's job, or a community member's well-being.

**2. We use AI to expand opportunity, especially for those without it.**
Rural students. ELL students. Special-ed students. First-generation small-business owners. Native communities. AI that doesn't reach them isn't worth the electricity.

**3. We govern AI like we engineer it — with checklists, evidence, and reviews.**
Every district, every business, every nonprofit in NCW commits to a written, public AI use policy. Even if it's 5 sentences.

**4. We protect critical thinking, not just outcomes.**
If a tool makes our kids better at the test but worse at thinking, it fails. We measure both.

**5. We disclose. Always.**
When AI helped write it, we say so. We teach our kids to do the same.

**6. We triangulate hard questions.**
No single AI gets to answer a question that matters. We use Model Councils, second opinions, and Deep Think for anything important.

**7. We keep the receipts.**
Prompts saved. Outputs saved. Edits saved. So a parent, a regulator, an auditor — or our own conscience — can verify our work.

### Why each point matters (the evidence behind the pledge)

**Why #1 (partner, not replacement):**
[MIT, June 2026](https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/2026/jun/19/chatbots-critical-thinking-skills): students who over-relied on AI to spot fake news had a **15.3% decline** in their ability to do it themselves after 4 weeks. AI without a human in the loop *atrophies* the very skills it's supposed to support.

**Why #2 (expand opportunity):**
[J-PAL evaluations and the npj Science of Learning 2025 systematic review](https://etcjournal.com/2026/06/02/ai-and-the-promise-of-educational-equity/): AI tutoring shows consistent gains for low-income learners and across diverse student populations. The biggest gains came in math and science — the subjects where students struggled most. *(Stanford GSE 6-month RCT of 10,000 students across 200 US schools: +34% test scores with AI tutors.)*

**Why #3 (govern like engineering):**
The [NIST AI Risk Management Framework](https://www.nist.gov/itl/ai-risk-management-framework) is the methodology the US government uses for AI risk. The AiGovOps Foundation translates it — and 40+ other global frameworks — into practical tools any organization can use for free.

**Why #4 (protect critical thinking):**
[Cornell, 95,000-student study, 2026](https://scientificinquirer.com/2026/05/26/widespread-ai-misuse-by-college-students-signals-need-to-rethink-assessment/): 1 in 3 college students regularly use GenAI for assignments; 9% admit to outright cheating. We need new assessments, not new bans.

**Why #5 (disclose always):**
The [draft AI Bill of Rights for Education](https://resources.norrageducation.org/resource/822/sneak-preview-a-blueprint-for-an-ai-bill-of-rights-for-education) is built on transparency. Students have a right to know when AI is grading them. Parents have a right to know when AI is teaching their kid. Customers have a right to know when AI is answering them.

**Why #6 (triangulate):**
Three frontier AIs in parallel — [Perplexity's Model Council](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/perplexity-ai_introducing-model-council-in-perplexity-activity-7425210595890769921-qRdh), [Gemini 3 Deep Think](https://blog.google/innovation-and-ai/models-and-research/gemini-models/gemini-3-deep-think/), and the [open-source AI Council Framework](https://github.com/focuslead/ai-council-framework) — consistently outperform any single model on hard questions. This isn't optional anymore. It's table stakes.

**Why #7 (keep the receipts):**
[Senate GAO investigation, June 2026](https://www.bluntrochester.senate.gov/news/press-releases/news-ranking-member-blunt-rochester-and-chair-tuberville-lead-investigation-into-ai-and-k-12-education/) into AI in K-12 schools — and the [FTC's amended COPPA Rule, effective April 22, 2026](https://aurascape.ai/answers/ai-compliance-frameworks-standards-and-governance-for-education-institutions/), expanding "personal information" to include biometrics — mean that "we didn't keep records" is no longer a defense.

### 🟢 STUDENT A — Maria

It's a Tuesday morning in 8th-grade algebra. Maria sits down with her Chromebook and opens **Khanmigo**, her AI tutor. It remembers what she got wrong yesterday. It doesn't give her the answer — it asks her a question, then another, then plays a 90-second video her teacher recorded for exactly this moment of confusion.

After 22 minutes a day for three months, Maria scores **34% higher** on her state-aligned algebra assessment than the students in the comparison group who used only standard video content.

Stanford Graduate School of Education followed 10,000 students like Maria across 200 US schools for six months. The biggest gains came in math and science — the subjects where students struggled most.

Maria wants to be an engineer at the [Wenatchee Confluence dam](https://www.publicpower.com). For the first time, she thinks she can.

**Source:** [Stanford GSE 6-month RCT, June 2026](https://www.instagram.com/p/DZWP5QnFxuz/)
**Plus:** [Khan Academy's own data, ASU+GSV April 2026 — students using Khanmigo 30+ min/week showed +23% mastery on curriculum-aligned assessments.](https://www.aimscollaboratory.org/resources-all/how-khan-academy-is-building-a-better-ai-tutor-our-most-recent-learnings)

---

### 🔴 STUDENT B — Jake

It's Friday. Jake has had ChatGPT write his last seven English essays. His teacher knows something is wrong but can't prove it — the [detection tools are now sold by the same companies that sell the cheating tools](https://www.bostonglobe.com/2026/06/20/nation/student-cheating-is-becoming-impossible-detect-an-ai-era/).

In second period, a "nude" deepfake of Jake's classmate Sara is passed around in homeroom. It was generated in 4 minutes by a bored 14-year-old with a [free phone app](https://edtechmagazine.com/k12/article/2026/06/deepfakes-education-cyberbullying-age-ai-perfcon). Sara stops coming to school.

After 4 weeks of relying on a chatbot to tell him what's true online, Jake's own ability to spot fake news has dropped by **15.3%**. He doesn't notice. He never will, unless someone helps him.

His district just got a letter from the [US Senate Government Accountability Office](https://www.bluntrochester.senate.gov/news/press-releases/news-ranking-member-blunt-rochester-and-chair-tuberville-lead-investigation-into-ai-and-k-12-education/) asking how they're handling all of this.

### Why Maria's future isn't automatic

The same studies show: **whether you get Maria or Jake depends almost entirely on three things.**

**1. Whether there's a human in the loop.**
Maria has a teacher who reviews her tutor's path. Jake doesn't.

**2. Whether the school has a written AI policy.**
Maria's district has a 1-page policy that students see, parents see, and teachers follow. Jake's district has a 200-page binder no one read.

**3. Whether anyone is keeping the receipts.**
Maria's teacher saves the prompts, the AI's answers, and her edits in a shared Drive folder. So when a parent asks, the teacher answers in 60 seconds. Jake's school can't even tell you what AI tools are in use.

**That's it. That's the difference.**

### The 5-sentence AI policy that produces Maria's future

> At ____________________ (school / district / classroom), students may use AI for ____________________.
>
> Students must disclose ____________________.
>
> Teachers may use AI for ____________________ and will NOT use it for ____________________.
>
> The person responsible for AI use is ____________________.
>
> We review and update this policy every ____________________.

**Write yours. Post it. Sign the NCW Pledge.**

### What good looks like — three habits

🤝 **The two-eyes rule.** A human reviews anything that goes to a student, a parent, or a regulator.
🔍 **Triangulate hard questions.** No single AI answers a question that matters. Use a Model Council, a second opinion, or Deep Think.
📂 **Keep the receipts.** Save the prompt and the answer. You'll be glad you did.
