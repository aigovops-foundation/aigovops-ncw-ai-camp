### Q16 · What's the actual difference between Deep Think and standard Gemini — for a non-engineer?

**Tag-in:** [KEN]

**Answer (2–3 sentences):**
Standard Gemini gives you its first-best answer immediately — it's fast and usually fine for simple tasks. [Gemini 3 Deep Think](https://blog.google/innovation-and-ai/models-and-research/gemini-models/gemini-3-deep-think/) runs a visible reasoning trace first — you watch it consider multiple paths, check its own logic, surface assumptions, and explore edge cases before it commits to an answer — which is why it dramatically outperforms standard mode on multi-step, high-stakes questions like "design a 4-week intervention plan for a specific student." The analogy: standard Gemini is a smart person answering off the top of their head; Deep Think is that same smart person who asked for 3 minutes to think it through first.

**Citation/Source:** [Gemini 3 Deep Think, Google Blog](https://blog.google/innovation-and-ai/models-and-research/gemini-models/gemini-3-deep-think/)

### Q17 · What is a Model Council and how do I actually run one?

**Tag-in:** [KEN]

**Answer (2–3 sentences):**
A Model Council means asking the same hard question to three frontier AIs in parallel — Gemini Deep Think, Claude Opus extended thinking, GPT-5 reasoning — then pasting all three answers into a fourth tab with a synthesizer prompt that surfaces where they agree (high-confidence), where they disagree (worth your attention), what none of them said (often the most important thing), and what you should do first. Perplexity's Model Council runs this automatically in one interface; you can also do it manually in 15 minutes with three browser tabs and the synthesizer prompt we demoed today. The key principle is: no single AI gets to answer a question that matters — that's point 6 of the Pledge.

**Citation/Source:** [Gemini 3 Deep Think](https://blog.google/innovation-and-ai/models-and-research/gemini-models/gemini-3-deep-think/); [AIGovOps Foundation](https://www.aigovops-foundation.com)
