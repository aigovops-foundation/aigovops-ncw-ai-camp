About

Two builders, one foundation, and a region worth fighting for

The NCW AI Expo is a project of the AiGovOps Foundation in partnership with the NCW Tech Alliance — built by people who've shipped enterprise AI governance and who grew up in towns like the ones in this room.

Ken Johnston

Co-founder · Policy & Enterprise

Ken leads the policy and enterprise side of the Foundation — translating the way large organizations actually govern AI into something a small district or city can use on a Tuesday. His work at Microsoft, Ford, and Autonomic spanned the hard edges of regulated, large-scale AI: risk frameworks, model governance, and the unglamorous discipline of keeping the receipts.

At the Expo, Ken takes the regulatory and technical questions — NIST, the GAO investigation, data privacy, and what governance run like engineering really looks like.

Bob Rapp

Co-founder · Community & Classroom

Bob leads community translation — turning enterprise-grade governance into the five-sentence policy a teacher posts on the door and the AI-receipts folder a bakery owner starts on a Tuesday night. His career at Vodafone, IBM Watson, GE Healthcare, Microsoft, and GM taught him how technology reaches real people — and he grew up in Ellensburg, in the heart of Central Washington.

At the Expo, Bob takes the classroom-scale and NCW-regional questions — orchards, ELL students, small nonprofits, and what staying involved after today actually looks like.

The AiGovOps Foundation

A 501(c)(3) nonprofit on a mission to empower disadvantaged, rural, and small communities through improved AI access and impact.

Everything the Foundation builds is open-source and Apache-2.0 licensed — Beacon discovers the AI already in your environment, Umbrella translates 40-plus global frameworks into executable controls, and Lantern produces the human-readable report for a parent or a board. What costs seven figures in the enterprise is, here, your Saturday afternoon.

The partnership

In partnership with the NCW Tech Alliance

The NCW Tech Alliance has spent years building the technology community of North Central Washington — connecting the people, schools, and businesses who make this region work. The Expo is part of their AI in Action series, and the partnership keeps the conversation going long after August 11.
