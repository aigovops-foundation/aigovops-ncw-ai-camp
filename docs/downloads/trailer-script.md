## 5. PRODUCTION NOTES

### Visual Frames — Gamma Prompts
Each shot above includes a suggested Gamma prompt (see "Gamma prompt:" lines in the storyboard). General Gamma settings for this trailer:
- **Style:** Photorealistic documentary. Avoid anything that looks like corporate stock photography.
- **Color palette:** Warm amber, sage green, deep river blue — the natural palette of NCW. Avoid cool-tech blues and gradients that read "startup."
- **People:** Diverse, authentic, not model-looking. Latina students, orchard-worker demographics, small-town civic energy. Representation matters in NCW.
- **Lighting:** Golden hour and warm indoor light throughout. No harsh studio lighting.
- For the hero shot (Shot 9), Gamma's **"cinematic wide"** preset + **"dramatic stage lighting"** modifier produces the best result. Generate 4 variants and pick the one where the audience body language reads most clearly.

---

### ElevenLabs Voice Settings

**YOUNG VOICE (opener — shots 1–2 and mid-section shot 4)**
- Recommended voice ID: **Elli** (youthful, clear, slightly breathy — reads as genuine, not precocious)
- Alternative: **Aria** (warmer, slightly older-sounding)
- Style: Conversational, not theatrical. This is a real kid narrating, not an ad.
- Stability: **0.65** (some natural variation — avoid robotic evenness)
- Clarity: **0.80** (crisp consonants for the short, punchy lines)
- Speed: **0.95x** (slightly slower than default — let the images breathe)
- Emotion note: Curious, slightly wondering. Not inspirational-ad earnest.

**ADULT VOICE (main narrator — shots 3, 5–12)**
- Recommended voice ID: **Adam** (warm, authoritative, NPR-adjacent — exactly Ken/Bob energy)
- Alternative: **Daniel** (slightly younger, more conversational pace)
- Style: Confident but not hard-sell. This is a trusted community member, not a pitch person.
- Stability: **0.72** (slight warmth variance — avoid monotone)
- Clarity: **0.75** (warmer than the young voice — more room in the mid-frequencies)
- Speed: **0.92x** (measure the close sections — "This community doesn't wait. It builds." should feel like it means something)
- Emotion note: The close (shots 10–12) should be noticeably slower and warmer than the pitch-energy section (shots 6–8). ElevenLabs SSML tag `<break time="0.4s"/>` between "This community doesn't wait." and "It builds." recommended.

**Pacing overall:** Medium-fast through the montage section (shots 4–8). Breathe in shots 1–3 (the Maria opening) and shots 9–12 (the close). The energy curve: quiet wonder → building momentum → full conviction → quiet landing.

### Music — 3 Royalty-Free Track Styles

**Option 1 — Orchestral hopeful (recommended for full-length trailer)**
Style: Solo piano opening, cello enters at shot 3, light strings/percussion build through the montage, full orchestral resolve at the final card. Think: a small-town version of a Hans Zimmer underscore.
- Search on: Artlist, Musicbed, Epidemic Sound
- Artlist tags: "hopeful orchestral" + "cinematic" + "uplifting"
- Specific track recommendation: Artlist's *"Home"* by Ran Raiten or similar (solo piano → full strings arc)
- Key cue: the piano must be present in shots 1–2 (Maria) and must return in shots 10–11 (the close). It's the emotional anchor.

**Option 2 — Acoustic indie (better for social short-form versions)**
Style: Fingerpicked acoustic guitar, simple percussion, authentic Pacific Northwest feel. Sufjan Stevens meets community radio.
- Search on: Artlist or SoundStripe, tag: "acoustic indie" + "community" + "warm"
- This works especially well for the 30-second Hook C cut (the people-focused hook)
- Avoids the risk of the orchestral version reading as "corporate inspiration"

**Option 3 — Cinematic uplifting (backup, good for the $5K/prize section)**
Style: Driving momentum track — building percussion, light synth undertone, resolves warmly. Not electronic; not pop. Think Explosions in the Sky without the post-rock drift.
- Artlist tags: "cinematic uplifting" + "building" + "inspiring"
- Best used as a single-track replacement if Option 1 feels too understated for the Expo's specific audience
